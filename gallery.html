Image Gallery

Discover the majestic narwhal through our collection of stunning images. Each photo captures a unique aspect of these fascinating creatures in their Arctic habitat.

The Iconic Tusk

A male narwhal surfaces, displaying its magnificent spiral tusk extending from its upper jaw. This tusk is actually an elongated tooth that can grow up to 10 feet in length.

Social Gathering

A pod of narwhals swimming together in the Arctic waters. Narwhals are highly social creatures, typically traveling in groups of 5-10, though gatherings of hundreds have been observed during migrations.

Tusk Detail

Close-up view of the narwhal's spiral tusk. The distinctive left canine tooth has a counterclockwise spiral and contains millions of nerve endings, suggesting it may serve as a sensory organ.

Arctic Adaptation

A narwhal navigating through icy Arctic waters. These whales are perfectly adapted to life in freezing conditions, with specialized features like thick blubber and the ability to navigate under ice.

Deep Diving Hunter

Narwhals can dive to depths of over 1,500 meters (4,920 feet) to hunt for prey. They feed primarily on Greenland halibut, Arctic cod, squid, and shrimp found near the ocean floor.

Mother and Calf

A narwhal mother with her young calf. Female narwhals typically give birth every three years, and calves stay with their mothers for about 20 months, learning essential survival skills.
